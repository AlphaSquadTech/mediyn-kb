# Tips for Appointment & Session Scheduling

## Recommendations

- **Set up session types first** -- Before you start scheduling, define all the session types your practice offers. Include accurate procedure codes, durations, and rates.

- **Use recurring schedules for regular patients** -- If you see a patient weekly, set up a recurring schedule instead of creating individual sessions each time. This saves time and reduces errors.

- **Keep availability templates current** -- Update your availability templates whenever your schedule changes. This ensures online booking shows accurate time slots.

- **Set a buffer between sessions** -- Use the buffer time setting in your booking configuration. Even 10-15 minutes between sessions prevents scheduling overlaps and gives you transition time.

- **Create a clear cancellation policy** -- Define your notice period and fees upfront. This sets patient expectations and protects your practice from lost revenue.

- **Review your daily queue each morning** -- The daily queue gives you a quick view of your sessions for the day, organized by time. Make it part of your morning routine.

- **Use session packages to encourage commitment** -- Offering prepaid packages can improve patient retention and simplify billing.

- **Enable intake on booking** -- Turn on the option to send intake forms automatically when a booking is confirmed. This gets paperwork out of the way before the first session.

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I schedule a session for a patient I am not assigned to?**
A: No. Mediyn requires that the therapist be assigned to the patient before a session can be created. This ensures continuity of care.

**Q: What happens if a patient books a time that conflicts with another session?**
A: Mediyn prevents scheduling conflicts. Available slots are calculated after accounting for existing sessions and pending booking requests.

**Q: Can I move a session from one therapist to another?**
A: Not directly. Cancel the original session and create a new one with the other therapist.

**Q: How far in advance does Mediyn create recurring sessions?**
A: Up to 90 days. You can manually generate additional sessions if you need to extend further.

**Q: What is the difference between canceling and archiving a session?**
A: Canceling is for sessions that will not take place. Archiving is for completed sessions you want to move to long-term storage.

**Q: Can patients cancel their own bookings?**
A: Yes. Patients can cancel their booking requests. Your cancellation policy determines whether a fee applies.

**Q: Do I need to enable online booking?**
A: No. Online booking is optional. You can continue to schedule all sessions manually through Mediyn.
