# How to Administer Assessments

Mediyn makes it easy to assign clinical screeners to your patients, whether as a one-time check-in or based on a smart recommendation.

## Getting Assessment Recommendations

1. Open the session for the patient you want to assess.
2. Choose **Get Assessment Recommendations**.
3. Mediyn analyzes the session's clinical notes and the patient's assessment history to suggest which instruments to administer.

You can also:
- **Limit the number of suggestions** -- Set a maximum number of recommended instruments
- **Add a session note** -- Provide extra context to guide the recommendations

### What to Expect

- Mediyn returns a list of recommended instruments with explanations.
- You always make the final decision. Mediyn suggests but never auto-assigns.
- You can accept a recommendation (which creates an assessment assignment) or dismiss it.

## Accepting or Dismissing Recommendations

- **Accept**: Choose **Accept** on a recommendation. Mediyn creates an assessment assignment for the patient automatically.
- **Dismiss**: Choose **Dismiss** if the recommendation is not appropriate right now.

You can view all past recommendations and their status (pending, accepted, or dismissed) at any time. You can narrow down by patient or session.

## Creating a One-Time Assessment

1. Open the patient's profile.
2. Choose **Create Assessment**.

You'll need to provide:
- **Instrument** -- The screening tool you want to use (e.g., PHQ-9, GAD-7)

You can also:
- **Link to a session** -- Connect the assessment to a specific session
- **Set a due date** -- Give the patient a deadline
- **Add notes** -- Include context that will be shown to the patient

### What to Expect

- The assessment is created in the **Assigned** stage.
- The patient sees it in their Mediyn account and can begin answering questions.
- All questions must be answered before the patient can submit. Partial submissions are not accepted.

## Viewing All Assessments

You can browse assessments in several ways:

- **By patient** -- See all assessments for a specific patient, with options to narrow down by status or instrument
- **Across patients** -- View all assessments across your caseload
- **By status** -- Show only assigned, in progress, completed, or expired assessments

Clinic administrators can view all assessments across the practice and narrow down by therapist.

## Good to Know

- Each assessment records which therapist assigned it and how the attribution was determined (directly assigned, linked through a session, or manually set).
- Patients see only their own assessments. Therapists see assessments for their assigned patients.
- If a patient does not complete an assessment by the due date, it moves to the **Expired** stage.
