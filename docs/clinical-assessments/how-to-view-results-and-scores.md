# How to View Results and Scores

When a patient completes an assessment, Mediyn automatically scores it and provides severity levels, subscale breakdowns, and trend analysis.

## Viewing a Completed Assessment

1. Open the patient's profile or the assessments list.
2. Find the completed assessment and select it.

### What to Expect

- **Total score** -- The overall score calculated from the patient's answers
- **Severity level** -- A label indicating the clinical severity (e.g., minimal, mild, moderate, severe)
- **Severity label** -- A plain-language description of what the score means
- **Subscale scores** -- If the instrument has subscales, you see individual scores for each area
- **Instrument details** -- The full set of questions, scoring method, and defined score ranges
- **Patient's answers** -- The specific responses the patient submitted
- **Completion date and time** -- When the patient finished the assessment

## Understanding Score Ranges

Each instrument defines its own score ranges and severity levels. For example, a PHQ-9 might define:

- 0-4: Minimal depression
- 5-9: Mild depression
- 10-14: Moderate depression
- 15-19: Moderately severe depression
- 20-27: Severe depression

Mediyn maps the patient's total score to the correct range automatically.

## Tracking Progress Over Time

1. Open the patient's profile.
2. Choose **Assessment Progress** and select an instrument.

### What to Expect

- A time-series view of the patient's scores across all completed assessments for that instrument.
- A **trend indicator** showing whether the patient is:
  - **Improving** -- Scores are trending in a positive direction
  - **Worsening** -- Scores are trending in a concerning direction
  - **Stable** -- Scores are holding steady
  - **Insufficient data** -- Not enough completed assessments to determine a trend

Patients can also view their own score history and trends from their account.

## Viewing Aggregate Clinical Outcomes (Administrators)

Clinic administrators can view aggregate outcomes across the practice.

You can narrow down by:
- **Instrument** -- Focus on a specific screening tool
- **Date range** -- Look at a specific time period
- **Therapist** -- Compare outcomes by provider

### What to Expect

- Total number of assessments administered
- Average score across all patients
- Number of patients improving
- Number of patients worsening

## Good to Know

- Auto-scoring happens instantly when a patient submits. There is no waiting period.
- The patient's answers are only visible after submission. In-progress entries are not shown to therapists.
- Progress tracking requires at least two completed assessments for the same instrument to show a trend.
