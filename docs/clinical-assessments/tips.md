# Tips for Clinical Assessments & Screeners

## Recommendations

- **Start with Mediyn's recommendations** -- Let Mediyn suggest which instruments to administer based on your session notes. It saves time and ensures clinical relevance.
- **Use recurring schedules for ongoing treatment** -- Set up recurring assessments (e.g., PHQ-9 every two weeks) to build a clear picture of progress over time.
- **Set up severity alerts early** -- Create alert rules for critical thresholds before you start administering assessments. This way you are notified from day one.
- **Add notes when assigning** -- Include a brief message for the patient explaining why the assessment matters. This can improve completion rates.
- **Review progress trends regularly** -- Check the trend indicator (improving, stable, worsening) to guide treatment decisions during sessions.
- **Combine multiple instruments** -- Use different screeners to get a well-rounded clinical picture. For example, pair a depression screener with an anxiety screener.
- **Act on alerts promptly** -- When you receive a severity alert, review the patient's full score history before your next session.

## Common Questions

**Q: What instruments are available?**
A: Mediyn includes validated tools across many clinical domains: depression (e.g., PHQ-9), anxiety (e.g., GAD-7), trauma, suicide risk, substance use, ADHD, functional impairment, OCD, bipolar, pediatric, and multi-domain screeners.

**Q: Can a patient partially complete an assessment?**
A: Patients must answer all questions before submitting. They cannot submit a partial assessment.

**Q: What happens when an assessment expires?**
A: If a patient does not complete the assessment by the due date, it moves to the Expired stage. You can create a new one if needed.

**Q: How is the therapist attribution determined?**
A: Mediyn records the therapist linked to the assessment based on the assignment, the session, or a manual selection. This ensures accurate reporting even if patient-therapist assignments change.

**Q: Can patients see their own scores?**
A: Yes. Patients can view their completed assessments, scores, severity levels, and score history from their own account.

**Q: Do severity alerts trigger any automatic actions?**
A: No. Alerts are notifications only. You always decide how to respond clinically.

**Q: Can clinic administrators see assessments across therapists?**
A: Yes. Administrators can view all assessments across the practice and narrow down by therapist, patient, instrument, or status.
