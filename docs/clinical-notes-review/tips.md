# Tips for Clinical Notes & Review

## Recommendations

- **Review notes promptly** -- Try to review and approve clinical notes within 24 hours of the session. Details are freshest in your mind, and it keeps your documentation current.

- **Start with key insights** -- Review the key insights first. Red flags and suggested follow-up questions can help you recall important moments before you read the full transcription.

- **Edit before you approve** -- Once an artifact is approved, it is finalized. Make all your corrections and additions while the note is still in the "In Review" stage.

- **Use version history for accountability** -- Every edit is tracked with who made the change and when. This creates a reliable record for compliance and auditing purposes.

- **Review red flags carefully** -- The key insights section highlights potential concerns automatically. Always take a close look at flagged items. They may inform your treatment plan.

- **Approve artifacts one at a time** -- You do not need to approve everything at once. Approve the transcription when it looks good, then take time with the summary and insights separately.

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I undo an approval?**
A: No. Once an artifact is approved, it is final. Review thoroughly before approving.

**Q: What if the generated notes are inaccurate?**
A: Edit the content directly. Mediyn saves the original as a version, and your corrected content becomes the current version.

**Q: Can other therapists see my edits?**
A: Clinic administrators and the assigned therapist can view clinical notes and version history for sessions in their practice.

**Q: Are the generated notes a replacement for my own clinical judgment?**
A: No. Mediyn generates notes as a starting point. Always review and edit them to reflect your professional assessment accurately.

**Q: How do I know which artifacts still need my review?**
A: Check the state of each artifact. Items marked "In Review" still need your attention. Items marked "Approved" are finalized.

**Q: Does editing a note change the original generated version?**
A: No. The original is preserved in the version history. Your edit creates a new current version while keeping the original intact.
