# How to Manage Good Faith Estimates

Create and send Good Faith Estimates (GFEs) to patients as required by the No Surprises Act.

## Steps

### Creating a Good Faith Estimate

1. Open the patient's profile in Mediyn.
2. Select the option to create a new Good Faith Estimate.
3. Fill in the required details.

**You'll need to provide:**

- Provider name
- Provider NPI number
- Diagnosis codes
- Service lines (the planned treatments and their costs)
- Start date for the estimate
- End date for the estimate

**You can also:**

- Link the GFE to an existing intake packet

4. Save and send the estimate to the patient.

### Patient acknowledgment

1. The patient receives the Good Faith Estimate.
2. They review the estimated costs and service details.
3. They acknowledge the estimate in Mediyn.

## What to Expect

After you create a GFE, it moves through these stages:

- **Draft** -- Created but not yet sent to the patient
- **Sent** -- Delivered to the patient for review
- **Acknowledged** -- The patient has reviewed and acknowledged the estimate
- **Expired** -- The estimate is no longer valid

Mediyn records the date and time when the patient acknowledges the estimate. The GFE includes a total estimated cost calculated from your service lines.

## Good to Know

- The No Surprises Act requires you to provide a GFE to uninsured and self-pay patients before scheduled services.
- You can link a GFE to an intake packet so the patient receives everything together.
- Patients can view their pending GFEs alongside their other intake forms.
- Each GFE has a validity window defined by the start and end dates you set.
- Keep diagnosis codes and service lines accurate. The total cost is calculated from your entries.
