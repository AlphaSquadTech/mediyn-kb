# Tips for Intake Forms & Consent

## Recommendations

- **Send packets early** -- Give patients enough time to complete forms before their appointment. A 7-day expiration window works well for most practices.

- **Bundle forms together** -- Include all required consent forms and intake questionnaires in a single packet. This saves the patient from receiving multiple separate requests.

- **Clone before customizing** -- If you like a system template but need to tweak it, clone it first. This keeps the original intact and gives you a custom version to edit.

- **Use categories consistently** -- Assign a category to every template you create. This makes it easier to find the right template when building packets.

- **Require signatures on consent forms** -- Always enable the signature requirement on consent-type forms. This creates a clear record that the patient agreed.

- **Monitor packet status regularly** -- Check for partially completed or expired packets. Follow up with patients who have not finished their forms.

- **Keep GFEs up to date** -- If treatment plans change, create a new Good Faith Estimate with updated service lines and costs.

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I edit a system template?**
A: No. System templates are read-only. To customize one, clone it first. The clone becomes your own editable template.

**Q: What happens when a packet expires?**
A: The patient can no longer access the forms. You can send a new packet with the same templates if needed.

**Q: Can I include a Good Faith Estimate in an intake packet?**
A: Yes. When you create a GFE, you can link it to an intake packet so the patient receives everything together.

**Q: How do I know when a patient finishes their forms?**
A: Check the packet status. It will show as "Completed" when all forms in the packet have been submitted.

**Q: Can patients use their phone to sign consent forms?**
A: Yes. Patients can sign using either a checkbox confirmation or by drawing their signature on any device.

**Q: What if I need to archive a template that is in use?**
A: Templates included in active intake packets cannot be archived. Wait until those packets are completed or expired first.
