# Tips for Patient Login & Portal Access

## Recommendations

- **Send invitations early** — Invite patients to the portal before their first session so they can complete intake forms ahead of time.
- **Confirm the email address** — Make sure you have the patient's correct email before sending a magic link. A typo means the link goes to the wrong inbox.
- **Encourage biometric sign-in** — Let patients know they can use fingerprint or face recognition for faster access on their phone.
- **Revoke lost devices promptly** — If a patient reports a lost or stolen device, remove it from their trusted list right away to protect their data.
- **Guide patients through the portal** — During their first session, walk patients through the portal so they know where to find worksheets, messages, and invoices.
- **Remind patients to complete worksheets before sessions** — Completed worksheets give you better context for the upcoming appointment.
- **Use the portal for between-session communication** — Encourage patients to message you through the portal instead of using personal email or phone.

## Common Questions

**Q: What happens if a patient's magic link expires?**
A: The patient can request a new magic link from the sign-in page. Expired links cannot be reused.

**Q: Can a patient access the portal from multiple devices?**
A: Yes. Each device is added to their trusted device list when they sign in. You can see all devices in the patient's record.

**Q: What if a patient does not have a smartphone?**
A: Patients can access the portal from any web browser on a computer or tablet. Biometric sign-in is not available on web, but magic links work on all devices.

**Q: Can I deactivate a patient's portal access?**
A: Yes. Changing a patient's app access status to "Deactivated" prevents them from signing in. You can reactivate it later.

**Q: Do patients need to create an account?**
A: No. When you send an invitation and the patient clicks the magic link, Mediyn creates their access automatically. There is no sign-up form or password to set.

**Q: Can patients see their assessment scores?**
A: Yes. After submitting an assessment, patients see their total score, severity level, and score history over time.

**Q: What signature options do patients have for consent forms?**
A: Patients can sign by checking a checkbox or by drawing their signature on screen.
