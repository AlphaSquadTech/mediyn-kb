# Patient Records

Mediyn helps you create, organize, and maintain patient records so you can focus on care.

![Patient Records](../images/clinic/patients.png)

## What You Can Do

- Add new patient records with contact and consent details
- View and search your patient list
- Update patient profiles as information changes
- Assign therapists to patients
- Archive patients who are no longer active and restore them if needed
- Invite patients to the Mediyn patient portal
- Send intake packets to new patients
- View detailed patient summaries including session history and assessments

## Key Concepts

- **Patient record** — The complete profile for a patient, including name, contact information, consent status, and clinical details.
- **Display name** — The name shown throughout Mediyn. If a preferred name is set, it is used. Otherwise, the first and last name are combined.
- **Patient status** — Each patient record has a status:
  - **Active** — The patient is currently receiving care.
  - **Archived** — The patient record is stored but hidden from everyday views.
- **Consent status** — Tracks whether the patient has given consent:
  - **Pending** — Consent has been requested but not yet received.
  - **Obtained** — The patient has given consent.
  - **Revoked** — The patient has withdrawn consent.
- **App status** — Tracks whether the patient has access to the Mediyn patient portal:
  - **Not invited** — No invitation has been sent.
  - **Invited** — An invitation was sent but not yet accepted.
  - **Active** — The patient is using the portal.
  - **Deactivated** — Portal access has been removed.
- **Intake status** — Tracks the progress of intake paperwork:
  - **Not sent** — No intake packet has been sent.
  - **Sent** — The intake packet was sent to the patient.
  - **Partially completed** — The patient has started but not finished the forms.
  - **Completed** — All intake forms are finished.
  - **Expired** — The intake packet is no longer valid.
- **Therapist assignment** — Links a patient to one or more therapists for care management.
- **Risk flags** — Indicators added to a patient record to highlight important clinical considerations.
