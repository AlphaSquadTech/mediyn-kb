# Tips for Patient Records

## Recommendations

- **Add preferred names when applicable** — If a patient goes by a different name, set the preferred name so your team always sees the right name.
- **Track consent status carefully** — Update the consent status as soon as consent is obtained or revoked. This keeps your records accurate for compliance.
- **Use risk flags proactively** — Add risk flags to highlight important clinical considerations. This helps the entire care team stay informed.
- **Send intake packets early** — Invite patients to the portal and send intake forms before their first appointment. This saves time during the visit.
- **Archive instead of ignoring** — When a patient is no longer active, archive their record. This keeps your active list manageable without losing any data.
- **Review assignments regularly** — Make sure each patient is assigned to the right therapist, especially after staffing changes.

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I delete a patient record?**
A: Mediyn uses archiving instead of deletion. Archived records are hidden from your active list but all data is preserved.

**Q: Who can see a patient's record?**
A: Therapists can only see patients assigned to them. Clinic administrators can see all patients.

**Q: What happens to a patient's data when they are archived?**
A: Nothing is lost. All records, notes, and session history remain intact and accessible through the archived view.

**Q: Can I assign a patient to more than one therapist?**
A: Yes. Clinic administrators can assign multiple therapists to a single patient.

**Q: How do I know if a patient has completed their intake forms?**
A: Check the patient's intake status. It will show as Not Sent, Sent, Partially Completed, Completed, or Expired.

**Q: What is the difference between the patient list and the detailed patient view?**
A: The patient list shows basic information for all patients. The detailed view shows a comprehensive summary including session history, assessments, scores, and upcoming appointments.
