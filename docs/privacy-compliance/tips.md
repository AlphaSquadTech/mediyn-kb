# Tips for Privacy & Compliance

## Recommendations

- **Review audit logs regularly** — Do not wait for an incident. Schedule monthly reviews of your audit trail to catch any unusual activity early.

- **Run access recertification quarterly** — Team members come and go. A quarterly review ensures only current, authorized staff have access to patient data.

- **Set appropriate inactivity timeouts** — The default is 30 minutes. For practices in shared office spaces, consider a shorter timeout for added security.

- **Require re-verification for sensitive actions** — Configure your security policies so that actions like approving clinical records or exporting PDFs require identity confirmation.

- **Keep consent status up to date** — Update consent indicators promptly when patients provide or withdraw consent. This protects both you and your patients.

- **Use PHI masking to limit data exposure** — Ensure that staff members only see the patient information relevant to their role. Front-desk staff likely do not need access to clinical notes.

- **Document your compliance processes** — Use Mediyn's audit trail and recertification history as evidence of your ongoing compliance efforts.

## Common Questions

**Q: Can audit log entries be deleted or changed?**
A: No. The audit trail is unchangeable by design. This ensures a reliable record for compliance and legal purposes.

**Q: How often should I run access recertification?**
A: At least quarterly is recommended. Practices with frequent staff changes may benefit from monthly reviews.

**Q: What happens if I set the inactivity timeout too short?**
A: Team members will be asked to sign in more frequently. This improves security but may feel inconvenient. Find a balance that works for your practice.

**Q: Who can update security policies?**
A: Only clinic administrators can view and change security policies, including inactivity timeouts and re-verification requirements.

**Q: What is the difference between redaction policies and PHI masking?**
A: Redaction policies control how sensitive data is masked or hidden during processing. PHI masking controls which fields are visible to different roles in your practice. Both work together to protect patient privacy.

**Q: Is consent tracking required?**
A: Tracking consent is a compliance best practice. Mediyn makes it easy by letting you record and update consent status at the session level.
