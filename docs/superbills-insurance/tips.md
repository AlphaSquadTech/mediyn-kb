# Tips for Superbills & Insurance Claims

## Recommendations

- **Enable automatic superbill generation** — In your billing settings, turn on auto-create superbills. Mediyn will generate a superbill every time a session is approved, saving you manual effort.

- **Double-check diagnosis codes before finalizing** — Once a superbill is finalized, it cannot be edited. Verify that your ICD-10 codes are accurate before you lock it in.

- **Keep your provider details current** — Your NPI, name, and tax identification number appear on every superbill. Update them in your profile if anything changes.

- **Ensure patient insurance info is on file** — Superbills are most useful when they include the patient's insurance details. Add this information to the patient's profile before creating superbills.

- **Finalize superbills promptly** — Do not leave superbills in Draft status for too long. Finalize them so patients can submit to their insurance without delay.

- **Use consistent procedure codes** — Set the correct CPT code for each session type in Mediyn. This pre-fills automatically and reduces errors.

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I edit a superbill after finalizing it?**
A: No. Finalized superbills are locked. If you need to make a correction, void the superbill and create a new one.

**Q: Can I create a superbill for a session that was not approved?**
A: Superbills are designed for completed, approved sessions. The session should be in an approved state before generating a superbill.

**Q: What if my patient's insurance information is missing?**
A: Mediyn will still create the superbill, but the insurance section may be incomplete. The patient will need to add their insurance details before submitting the claim.

**Q: Who can void a superbill?**
A: Only clinic administrators can void superbills.

**Q: Does Mediyn submit the superbill directly to the insurance company?**
A: No. Mediyn generates the superbill document. Your patient submits it to their insurer for reimbursement.
