# Tips for Worksheets & Handouts

## Recommendations

- **Use recommendations first** -- Let Mediyn suggest the best template based on your session notes and the patient's assessment history. This saves time and improves clinical relevance.
- **Review warnings carefully** -- Warnings exist to protect patient privacy and content quality. Take a moment to read each one before acknowledging.
- **Set due dates for assignments** -- Giving patients a clear deadline encourages timely completion and helps you plan follow-up sessions.
- **Add session notes for better drafts** -- The more context you provide, the more personalized the worksheet draft will be.
- **Review responses promptly** -- Patients appreciate timely feedback. Mark assignments as reviewed soon after submission.
- **Use the replace feature instead of creating duplicates** -- If a worksheet needs corrections, replace the original rather than approving a second version for the same purpose.
- **Check the de-identification report** -- Before approving, review the report to make sure no patient-identifying information slipped through.

## Common Questions

**Q: Can I create more than one worksheet per session?**
A: Yes. You can approve multiple worksheets for the same session. Each one can address a different clinical concern.

**Q: What happens if I see a warning during rendering?**
A: Some warnings are informational and will not block you. Others require your acknowledgement. Review the flagged content, acknowledge the warning, and re-render the PDF.

**Q: Can a patient save their work and come back later?**
A: Yes. Patients can save their progress without submitting. Their responses are stored until they are ready to finalize.

**Q: What if the download link for my PDF has expired?**
A: You can get a new download link at any time from the approved worksheet. Links expire for security purposes.

**Q: Can clinic administrators see all worksheets?**
A: Yes. Clinic administrators can view all drafts and approved worksheets across the practice. Therapists only see worksheets for their assigned patients.

**Q: What does de-identification do?**
A: Mediyn scans the worksheet content for personal or identifying details and flags or removes them. This helps protect patient privacy when worksheets are shared.
